ClearScore Snaps Up Acre in Major Mortgage Tech Play

The fintech giant behind your customers' credit scores has acquired London-based Acre Platforms, a technology provider that serves mortgage and protection brokers. This follows ClearScore's earlier purchase of lender Aro Finance, signalling a serious push into the mortgage market.

Why it matters for UK mortgages: ClearScore now controls both customer data and broker technology platforms, potentially creating a powerful competitor that could reshape how mortgages are distributed and processed.

Armalytix Expands Beyond Conveyancing Checks

The data firm that handles anti-money laundering and affordability checks for lenders is broadening its focus to support estate agents and brokers earlier in the property transaction process. They've appointed a new head of sales to drive this expansion through 2026.

Why it matters for UK mortgages: Earlier integration of AML and affordability data could speed up mortgage applications and reduce the back-and-forth that currently slows down completions.

MAB Buys Data Firm for £2.8m as House Prices Jump

Mortgage Advice Bureau acquired data company Dashly whilst the property market saw its biggest January house price bounce in 25 years. The combination of tech investment and rising prices highlights the industry's current momentum.

Why it matters for UK mortgages: Large brokerages are investing heavily in data capabilities whilst strong house price growth suggests sustained mortgage demand ahead.

Study Shows AI Struggles with Real Office Work

New research tested leading AI systems on actual white-collar tasks from consulting, investment banking, and law. Most AI models failed when faced with complex, real-world professional work rather than simple, controlled tasks.

Why it matters for UK mortgages: Before investing in AI tools for underwriting or application processing, understand that current AI still struggles with the nuanced, complex decisions that mortgage professionals handle daily.

AI Agents Move into Core Business Operations

AI agents - software that can work independently rather than just respond to questions - are being deployed for lead generation, customer support, and financial processes. However, experts warn that giving AI too much autonomy without proper oversight could create operational chaos.

Why it matters for UK mortgages: AI agents could automate mortgage application processing and customer enquiries, but firms need robust data foundations and clear boundaries before deployment.
